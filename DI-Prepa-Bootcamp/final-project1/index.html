Rambam

Biography

Rabbi Moshe ben Maimon (also known as Rambam) was a prominent Jewish scholar of the Middle Ages. Born in Cairo, Egypt, he studied under the famous Rabbi Judah ha-Levi and later traveled to Spain where he became one of the leading figures of the Talmudic period.

He is best known for his magnum opus, the Mishneh Tor oth, a comprehensive commentary on the Talmud that remains an important text in Orthodox Judaism today.

Works


Mishneh Torah

Mishneh Torah: Maimonides composed the "Mishneh Torah," a comprehensive code of Jewish law. It gathered all binding laws from the Talmud and incorporated the positions of the Geonim. Despite initial opposition, it became a monumental contribution to the systemized writing of halakha (Jewish law).

Other Judaic and philosophical works

Maimonides authored various works in Judeo-Arabic, including a commentary on the Mishna, "Sefer Hamitzvot" listing the 613 mitzvot, "Sefer Ha'shamad" (Letter of Martyrdom), and the philosophical masterpiece "Guide for the Perplexed."

Medical Works

Maimonides was a notable figure in the medical field. His works include "Guide to Good Health," which addressed preventive medicine and public hygiene, and numerous treatises on topics such as hemorrhoids, asthma, and poisons.

The Oath of Maimonides

The Oath of Maimonides, a document on the medical calling, is recited as a substitute for the Hippocratic Oath. It emphasizes ethical principles and commitment to patient well-being.
